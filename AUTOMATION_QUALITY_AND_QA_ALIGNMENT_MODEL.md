# Automation Quality and QA Alignment Model

This document defines how the wider `lippytm` ecosystem can align automation with quality and quality assurance across reusable assets, study tracks, packs, workflow systems, and product candidates.

## Purpose
Help the ecosystem make automation support quality instead of bypassing it by connecting automated flow to review expectations, QA checks, supportability, and clearer reuse signals.

## Core Principle
Automation should strengthen quality by making important checks easier to repeat, easier to see, and easier to connect to promotion, rollout, and refresh decisions.

## Alignment Areas

### 1. Intake Quality Alignment
Connect:
- automated intake visibility
- minimum setup notes
- minimum review notes
- minimum audience and purpose clarity

### 2. Review Quality Alignment
Connect:
- setup review
- learning review
- security review
- support review
- duplication and simplification review

### 3. Promotion Quality Alignment
Connect:
- promotion into templates
- promotion into packs
- promotion into standards
- promotion into product candidates

### 4. Rollout Quality Alignment
Connect:
- readiness signals
- support expectations
- member-facing quality expectations
- affiliate-facing quality expectations
- product-facing quality expectations

### 5. Refresh Quality Alignment
Connect:
- support updates
- setup clarifications
- stale-pattern retirement
- stronger-replacement promotion
- portfolio and asset-family synchronization

## Quality Signals
A strong automation and QA connection exists when:
- automated flow does not skip core review signals
- support and refresh expectations are visible
- repeated weak patterns are simplified instead of multiplied
- maturity and readiness are easier to see
- automation reduces friction without hiding risk or confusion

## Best Practices
- automate visibility and repeatable checks before broad automation of rollout
- keep QA signals attached to the asset, family, and portfolio levels
- connect automation back to health checks, readiness models, and promotion models
- preserve experimental space while making mature quality paths easier to follow
- use automation to strengthen clarity, not to hide weak structure
