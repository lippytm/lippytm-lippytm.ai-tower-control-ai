# Automated Asset Flow Model

This document defines how the wider `lippytm` ecosystem can think about automated flow across reusable assets, study tracks, packs, guides, kits, and product candidates.

## Purpose
Help the ecosystem connect automation to review, promotion, rollout, support, and refresh decisions so growth becomes more repeatable and less ad hoc.

## Core Principle
Automation should strengthen clarity and reuse by helping assets move through visible stages instead of leaving every decision to isolated local judgment.

## Asset Flow Stages

### 1. Intake Stage
Use for:
- new lessons
- new study tracks
- new setup guides
- new packs or kits
- new workflow notes

Focus:
- record the purpose
- identify the audience
- attach setup and review notes

### 2. Review Stage
Use for:
- setup review
- learning review
- security review
- support review
- duplication review

Focus:
- confirm the asset is understandable
- confirm the asset reduces confusion
- confirm the asset fits the right family or portfolio

### 3. Promotion Stage
Use for:
- promotion into stronger guides
- promotion into templates
- promotion into packs
- promotion into standards

Focus:
- promote only durable repeated patterns
- simplify overlap before promoting broadly

### 4. Rollout Stage
Use for:
- local reuse
- cross-repo reuse
- member-facing reuse
- affiliate-facing reuse
- product-facing reuse

Focus:
- match rollout to readiness
- keep support expectations visible

### 5. Refresh Stage
Use for:
- support updates
- review updates
- setup clarifications
- retirement of weak versions
- promotion of stronger replacements

Focus:
- keep the ecosystem current
- simplify stale or overlapping patterns

## Best Practices
- automate visibility before automating broad rollout
- keep review signals attached to the asset itself
- use automation to reduce repeated friction, not hide complexity
- connect automated flow back to health checks, readiness, and promotion models
- preserve experimental space while making mature paths easier to follow
