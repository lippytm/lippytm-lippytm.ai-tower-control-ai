# Professional Review Handoff

## Purpose
This guide defines a standard handoff process for providing internal reporting materials to a qualified professional reviewer such as a CPA, tax preparer, accountant, EA, or tax attorney.

The goal is to reduce confusion, improve review efficiency, preserve audit trail clarity, and make recurring monthly, quarterly, and year-end handoffs easier.

## Handoff Goals
- provide a clear review packet
- state the purpose of review explicitly
- identify open questions and unresolved issues
- separate confirmed data from assumptions and estimates
- make supporting documents easy to locate
- preserve reviewer feedback for future periods

## Minimum Handoff Package
A standard professional-review handoff should include:
- Tax Return Audit Report
- Supporting Document Index
- Internal Controls Checklist
- Exception Summary
- relevant reconciliations
- intercompany schedule if relevant
- cost-center allocation schedule if relevant
- cover note with questions and deadlines

## Handoff Steps
### Step 1 — Prepare
Make sure the internal preparer has:
- updated report status
- completed document index as far as possible
- identified missing items
- identified open questions
- flagged items needing professional judgment

### Step 2 — Assemble
Bundle the relevant reports, schedules, and supporting references in a consistent order.

### Step 3 — Label
Clearly label:
- draft vs reviewed items
- estimates vs confirmed values
- high-priority issues
- deadlines and filing-related time sensitivity

### Step 4 — Deliver
Provide the packet through the chosen method and preserve a record of what was sent and when.

### Step 5 — Capture Feedback
Log reviewer comments, requested changes, and follow-up items.

### Step 6 — Resolve and Archive
Update the packet, mark resolutions, and archive the reviewed version with a timestamp.

## Recommended Review Questions
Use clear prompts such as:
- are these classifications reasonable?
- do these unresolved items require further documentation?
- do any owner or intercompany items need special treatment?
- are any assumptions inappropriate or risky?
- what should be corrected before final filing or archival?

## Best Practices
- ask specific questions, not vague requests
- do not hide uncertainties
- preserve version history for each handoff
- keep a repeatable packet order every period
- carry unresolved feedback into the next period's workflow where needed
