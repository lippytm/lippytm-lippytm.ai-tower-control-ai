# Synthetic Intelligence Orchestration

This document defines how the Control Tower should orchestrate Synthetic Intelligence and Super Synthetic Intelligence systems across the lippytm fleet.

---

## Purpose

The Control Tower should become the governing layer for:

- SI mission coordination
- Super SI supervision
- lane-aware orchestration
- cross-repo event normalization
- governed self-coding proposals

---

## Orchestration Roles

### Control Tower as SI Conductor
Responsible for:
- mission intake
- target selection
- policy evaluation
- rollout sequencing
- telemetry aggregation
- escalation and rollback coordination

### MyClaw as Swarm Fabric
Responsible for:
- task routing
- agent coordination
- dead-letter handling
- supervisor flows

### Specialist Repos as Domain Surfaces
Responsible for domain-specific execution in revenue, commerce, product, knowledge, and lab lanes.

---

## Suggested SI Mission Types

- repo upgrade mission
- documentation manufacturing mission
- revenue funnel optimization mission
- commerce activation mission
- assistant escalation mission
- knowledge packaging mission
- promotion review mission

---

## Super SI Mission Loop

1. observe repo and business signals
2. classify lane and sensitivity
3. decompose into specialist tasks
4. dispatch to correct swarm cluster
5. monitor quality and telemetry
6. escalate or rollback when needed
7. summarize learning into standards and future missions

---

## Best Practices

- keep orchestration event-driven
- keep domain execution inside the owning lane
- keep policy checks before protected actions
- keep telemetry rich enough for improvement loops
- keep human approval for high-impact changes

---

## Rule of thumb

Super SI becomes useful when orchestration is disciplined, lane-aware, and traceable rather than merely ambitious.
