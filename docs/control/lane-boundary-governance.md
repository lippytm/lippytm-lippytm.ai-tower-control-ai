# Lane Boundary Governance

This document explains how the Control Tower should govern cross-lane activity without erasing lane boundaries.

---

## Governance Goal

The Control Tower should coordinate the ecosystem, not flatten it.

That means:
- visibility across lanes
- orchestration across lanes
- policy across lanes
- but domain ownership staying within the correct lane

---

## Governance Rules

### Hub lane
Standards and policies remain authoritative here.

### Control lane
Coordinates missions, rollouts, approvals, and telemetry.

### Swarm lane
Executes and routes work without becoming the main policy source.

### Revenue lane
Owns public conversion paths and offer framing.

### Commerce lane
Owns money, access, and service-proof logic.

### Knowledge lane
Owns education, narrative, media, and concept packaging.

### Lab lane
Owns experimentation and proof-building.

---

## Control Tower Duties

The Control Tower may:
- read state from many lanes
- coordinate workflows across many lanes
- enforce policy checks
- trigger rollouts
- record telemetry

The Control Tower should avoid:
- becoming the long-term home for product-specific logic
- replacing commerce logic
- replacing creative ownership
- absorbing lab experimentation into production prematurely

---

## Best Practices

- coordinate across lanes, do not merge them mentally
- route work to the owning lane whenever possible
- record why cross-lane actions were taken
- use promotion rules before moving systems into new responsibility classes

---

## Rule of thumb

The Control Tower is strongest when it behaves like a conductor, not a giant pile of unrelated code.
