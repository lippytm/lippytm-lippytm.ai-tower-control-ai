# Self-Improvement Loops

This document defines governed self-improvement loops for Synthetic Intelligence and higher-order swarm systems across the lippytm ecosystem.

## Purpose

The ecosystem should improve continuously, but improvement must be:

- lane-aware
- policy-aware
- measurable
- reversible when needed
- useful to business and engineering outcomes

---

## Loop Stages

### 1. Observe
Collect events, telemetry, issue patterns, quality signals, and business signals.

### 2. Diagnose
Identify quality gaps, routing failures, monetization gaps, stale docs, or promotion blockers.

### 3. Recommend
Generate mission suggestions, fixes, safeguards, and upgrade candidates.

### 4. Govern
Apply RiskGate, protected-path checks, and review rules.

### 5. Execute
Run bounded changes through the correct lane and swarm cluster.

### 6. Measure
Check results using quality, business, and operational signals.

### 7. Summarize
Feed lessons into standards, templates, and future missions.

---

## Loop Types

- quality loop
- routing loop
- documentation loop
- monetization loop
- promotion loop
- rollback / recovery loop

---

## Best Practices

- keep loops narrow before scaling them
- tie each loop to measurable value
- preserve human approval for higher-impact change
- do not let experimentation bypass policy in production-oriented lanes
- promote successful loop patterns into manufacturing templates

---

## Rule of thumb

A strong self-improvement loop turns observed friction into governed improvement, not random churn.
