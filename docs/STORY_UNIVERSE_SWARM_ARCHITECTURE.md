# Story Universe Swarm Architecture

## Purpose
This document defines a swarm architecture for story universe development across the lippytm ecosystem, including storytelling systems, storyboards, storylines, concept continuity, media planning, educational-entertainment packaging, and creative development pipelines.

The goal is to let repositories, media systems, educational systems, and creative-business systems participate in one repeatable narrative manufacturing framework.

## Core Vision
Build a story-universe architecture that can support:
- long-form worldbuilding
- recurring themes and mythos continuity
- storyboards and scene planning
- educational-entertainment packaging
- documentary, trailer, movie, and music-film concept development
- repo-linked concept development and campaign narratives

## Core Narrative Layers
### Layer 1 — Concept Layer
Captures themes, motifs, archetypes, questions, settings, and recurring ideas.

### Layer 2 — Worldbuilding Layer
Defines story-world rules, organizations, systems, recurring settings, timelines, and conceptual logic.

### Layer 3 — Storyline Layer
Builds arcs, episodes, narrative branches, timelines, and sequence logic.

### Layer 4 — Storyboard Layer
Turns storylines into scenes, shot ideas, visual beats, asset needs, and production packets.

### Layer 5 — Packaging Layer
Converts narrative assets into trailers, documentaries, campaigns, educational media, and business-growth surfaces.

## Swarm Roles
### Concept Continuity Swarm
Tracks recurring themes, labels, and long-term concept continuity.

### Worldbuilding Swarm
Builds systems, organizations, settings, and rules.

### Storyline Swarm
Designs arcs, episodes, rerun structures, branches, and sequencing.

### Storyboard Swarm
Creates scene-level planning and visual narrative breakdowns.

### Media Packaging Swarm
Connects stories to trailers, documentaries, movies, music films, campaigns, and promotional materials.

## Suggested Domains
- Time Machines Management Systems
- Quantum Questions
- Many Worlds / rerun structures
- Space-alien themed educational-entertainment narratives
- Intergalactic zoological systems
- social-political satire / narrative systems in fictional worldbuilding contexts
- educational-business crossovers
- repo-linked media development

## Best Practices
- keep fictional worldbuilding clearly separated from real-world professional claims
- reuse one concept across docs, lessons, media, and campaign assets
- maintain continuity notes so recurring themes stay consistent
- organize narrative systems in modules that can grow over time
- connect story assets back to repo IDs, product IDs, and media packaging where useful
