# Hard and Soft Sciences Swarm R&D Model

## Purpose
This document defines a research-and-development model for applying Super Artificial DevOps Synthetic Intelligence Agents and Swarms across hard and soft sciences within the lippytm ecosystem.

## Core Goal
Create a repeatable system for turning scientific ideas, knowledge domains, educational assets, analytical methods, and cross-domain research questions into manufactured repo assets, products, services, applications, and learning systems.

## Science Domains
### Hard Sciences
- mathematics
- physics
- chemistry
- biology
- engineering
- computing systems
- data systems

### Soft Sciences
- psychology
- sociology
- economics
- political systems
- education systems
- organizational behavior
- communication and media analysis

## Core R&D Layers
### Layer 1 — Scientific Idea Layer
Questions, theories, hypotheses, methods, references, and domain concepts.

### Layer 2 — Structuring Layer
Research themes, analytical frameworks, comparison models, learning pathways, and experiment plans.

### Layer 3 — Manufacturing Layer
Templates, repo assets, packets, learning modules, AI solutions, dashboards, and applications.

### Layer 4 — Delivery Layer
Educational systems, reporting systems, product surfaces, media surfaces, business-growth applications, and platform packages.

### Layer 5 — Advancement Layer
Scorecards, maturity tracking, diagnostics, improvement loops, and cross-domain reuse.

## Swarm Roles
### Scientific Discovery Swarm
Finds high-value questions, topics, and research opportunities.

### Scientific Structuring Swarm
Turns scientific ideas into usable models, frameworks, and project candidates.

### Scientific Application Swarm
Converts scientific structures into repo assets, applications, learning systems, and other portfolio outputs.

### Scientific Documentation Swarm
Creates reusable docs, packets, modules, and traceability notes.

### Scientific Advancement Swarm
Promotes strong scientific assets into broader portfolio reuse and later waves.

## Best Practices
- separate scientific concepts from delivery packaging
- keep methods, hypotheses, and assumptions explicit
- connect scientific assets to real repo, product, and platform IDs
- reuse one scientific concept across education, analytics, media, and business surfaces where appropriate
- preserve review and governance notes when scientific outputs expand into public-facing or business-facing surfaces
