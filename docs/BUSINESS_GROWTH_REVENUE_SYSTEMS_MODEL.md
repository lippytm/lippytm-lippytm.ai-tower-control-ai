# Business Growth Revenue Systems Model

## Purpose
This document defines a revenue-systems model for the lippytm Business of Businesses portfolio so repositories, products, services, applications, AI solutions, educational assets, media assets, and CRM systems can be linked to clearer growth pathways.

## Core Goal
Create a structured portfolio model where repo assets can feed:
- product growth systems
- service growth systems
- application growth systems
- educational-business systems
- media and campaign systems
- CRM and follow-up systems
- reporting and accountability systems
- reusable portfolio bundles

## Core Revenue System Layers
### Layer 1 — Source Asset Layer
Repositories, components, concepts, learning modules, AI solutions, story assets, workflows, templates, and internal utilities.

### Layer 2 — Packaging Layer
Products, services, offers, applications, bundles, educational packages, AI-enabled systems, and delivery packages.

### Layer 3 — Delivery Layer
Websites, GitHub, dashboards, CRM systems, chat systems, campaign surfaces, and platform adapters.

### Layer 4 — Tracking Layer
Catalogs, trackers, scorecards, maturity notes, transparency notes, and review levels.

### Layer 5 — Expansion Layer
Cross-repo reuse, cross-domain growth, later-wave bundling, and stronger portfolio leverage.

## Suggested Revenue System Categories
- direct product pathways
- service-delivery pathways
- application pathways
- educational-business pathways
- media and campaign pathways
- CRM-assisted pathways
- AI-enabled solution pathways
- cross-domain bundle pathways

## Swarm Roles
### Revenue Mapping Swarm
Maps source assets to practical growth pathways.

### Packaging and Offer Swarm
Turns source assets into clearer deliverable structures.

### Delivery Fit Swarm
Matches packaged outputs to best-fit platforms and portfolio surfaces.

### Tracking and Feedback Swarm
Keeps scorecards, trackers, catalogs, and improvement notes current.

### Expansion Swarm
Finds reuse and bundle opportunities that strengthen multiple categories.

## Best Practices
- map growth pathways early, even if packaging is still evolving
- separate core assets from delivery-specific packaging
- prioritize systems that reinforce more than one category at once
- connect every pathway to stable IDs and tracker rows
- review portfolio revenue systems after each major wave
