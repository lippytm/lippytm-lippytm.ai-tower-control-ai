# R&D Skills and Capability Development Model

## Purpose
This document defines a model for manufacturing research-and-development skills, capabilities, and improvement pathways across the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to treat skills and capability growth as a structured production system that can support repositories, products, services, applications, educational systems, media systems, and Business of Businesses development.

## Core Vision
Build a skills-and-capability layer that can:
- identify the skills required by each repo, product, or application
- convert recurring work into trainable capability pathways
- map R&D learning into reusable templates, modules, and procedures
- strengthen innovation readiness across the full repo fleet
- connect capability growth to products, services, and applications

## Capability Layers
### Layer 1 — Skill Identification
Identify the skills needed for repo operations, product development, service delivery, media production, CRM systems, reporting systems, and swarm manufacturing.

### Layer 2 — Capability Structuring
Turn raw skill needs into structured capability areas, levels, and training sequences.

### Layer 3 — Applied Practice
Connect capabilities to exercises, projects, prototypes, templates, issue workflows, and deployments.

### Layer 4 — Reuse and Promotion
Turn proven capabilities into reusable teaching modules, profile patterns, and factory assets.

### Layer 5 — Portfolio Capability Growth
Track which capabilities are strongest, weakest, or most important for future waves.

## Suggested Capability Domains
- repo architecture and documentation
- DevOps and rollout operations
- integration mapping and synchronization
- CRM and growth workflows
- reporting and accountability systems
- educational and learning systems
- media and narrative production
- reasoning and diagnostics
- transparency and documentation governance

## Swarm Roles
### Skills Discovery Swarm
Finds capability gaps and emerging skill needs.

### Capability Structuring Swarm
Organizes skills into trainable capability paths.

### Applied Practice Swarm
Links capabilities to real repo and product work.

### Capability Promotion Swarm
Turns validated capabilities into reusable factory assets.

### Capability Tracking Swarm
Maintains capability status, maturity, and growth notes.

## Best Practices
- connect every important skill to real repo or product work
- prioritize capabilities that improve multiple areas at once
- reuse training and capability assets across repos whenever possible
- let capability growth feed product and service growth directly
- keep capability mapping linked to IDs, maturity, and wave priorities
