# Transparency Management Swarm Model

## Purpose
This document defines a transparency-management model for Super Artificial DevOps Synthetic Intelligence Agents and Swarms across the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to make swarm systems easier to understand, review, audit, explain, and govern by manufacturing transparency as a first-class operating layer.

## Core Vision
Support transparency functions for:
- role clarity
- scope clarity
- review-level visibility
- status visibility
- decision and recommendation traceability
- inventory and relationship visibility
- documentation of assumptions, limits, and next steps

## Core Layers
### Layer 1 — Role Transparency
Shows what a swarm or profile is supposed to do.

### Layer 2 — Status Transparency
Shows whether an object is planned, active, revised, archived, or under review.

### Layer 3 — Review Transparency
Shows what review level applies and who needs to review.

### Layer 4 — Traceability Transparency
Shows IDs, source objects, linked objects, and tracker references.

### Layer 5 — Explanation Transparency
Shows assumptions, limitations, unresolved issues, and next recommended actions.

## Swarm Roles
### Role Visibility Swarm
Keeps roles and scope boundaries explicit.

### Status Visibility Swarm
Maintains status trackers and maturity views.

### Review Visibility Swarm
Maintains review-level and escalation transparency.

### Traceability Visibility Swarm
Links IDs, registers, trackers, and relationship notes.

### Explanation Swarm
Converts system state into understandable summaries and transparency notes.

## Suggested Transparency Objects
- swarm profiles
- deployment profiles
- application catalog entries
- inventory objects
- sync entries
- quality scorecards
- maturity notes
- reasoning/diagnostics packets
- reporting packets

## Best Practices
- show scope, review level, and maturity on important objects
- make assumptions and limitations visible instead of hidden
- prefer explicit tracker links over implied status
- keep transparency notes brief but useful
- use transparency as an operating feature, not only a compliance feature
