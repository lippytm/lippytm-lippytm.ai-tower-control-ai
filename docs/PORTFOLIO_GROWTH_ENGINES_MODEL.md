# Portfolio Growth Engines Model

## Purpose
This document defines a portfolio-level growth-engines model for the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to identify and manufacture repeatable growth engines from repositories, products, services, applications, AI solutions, educational systems, media assets, CRM workflows, and reporting systems.

## Core Goal
Create a system where manufactured assets can contribute to one or more growth engines such as:
- product expansion
- service expansion
- application expansion
- educational-business expansion
- media and campaign expansion
- CRM and workflow expansion
- portfolio bundling and reuse expansion

## Growth Engine Layers
### Layer 1 — Source Assets
Repositories, components, concepts, learning modules, templates, prompts, AI solutions, story assets, and workflows.

### Layer 2 — Engine Packaging
Growth pathways formed as products, services, applications, bundles, offers, educational packages, or internal utilities.

### Layer 3 — Engine Delivery
Websites, GitHub, dashboards, CRM systems, chat surfaces, campaign/media surfaces, and platform adapters.

### Layer 4 — Engine Measurement
Scorecards, trackers, maturity, transparency notes, catalogs, and registers.

### Layer 5 — Engine Expansion
Cross-repo reuse, cross-domain reuse, later-wave promotion, and compounding portfolio leverage.

## Suggested Growth Engine Types
- repo growth engine
- product growth engine
- service growth engine
- application growth engine
- AI solution growth engine
- educational-business growth engine
- media/narrative growth engine
- CRM-assisted growth engine
- reporting-assisted growth engine

## Swarm Roles
### Growth Engine Discovery Swarm
Finds assets with compounding value potential.

### Growth Engine Packaging Swarm
Shapes assets into repeatable engine structures.

### Growth Engine Measurement Swarm
Keeps scorecards, trackers, and notes current.

### Growth Engine Expansion Swarm
Promotes strong engines into wider portfolio reuse.

## Best Practices
- define the engine logic clearly before scaling it
- keep source assets linked to package IDs and tracker rows
- prioritize engines that reinforce more than one category at once
- use measurement and maturity to pace rollout waves
- revisit growth engines after each wave or major product change
