# Repo Component and Concept Manufacturing Model

## Purpose
This document defines how to mass-manufacture Super Artificial DevOps Synthetic Intelligence Swarm Systems for repository components, sub-systems, concepts, ideas, features, and reusable product parts across the lippytm ecosystem.

## Core Vision
Treat each repository as a layered manufacturing surface containing:
- whole-repo applications
- reusable components
- concepts and idea modules
- product parts
- delivery surfaces
- storyline and media assets
- educational and business outputs

The factory should support manufacturing at every layer rather than only at the top-level repo layer.

## Manufacturing Layers
### Layer 1 — Repository Layer
The full repository as a manufacturing scope.

### Layer 2 — Product Layer
Products, services, offers, educational packages, media packages, or reporting packages linked to the repo.

### Layer 3 — Component Layer
Reusable pieces such as modules, workflows, templates, dashboards, content packs, story packets, CRM flows, or automation sub-systems.

### Layer 4 — Concept Layer
Ideas, themes, labels, strategic patterns, narrative concepts, educational topics, or worldbuilding objects that can later be turned into products or components.

## Mapping Questions
For each repo, product, component, or concept ask:
- what is the object ID?
- what value does it create?
- what higher-level repo or product is it linked to?
- what platforms deliver or support it?
- what swarm blueprints and profiles fit it?
- what review level applies?
- what can be reused elsewhere?

## Best Practices
- do not wait for full products to identify reusable components
- capture concepts early and map them to future component or product pathways
- let one concept support multiple outputs across education, media, business, and automation
- keep stable IDs at every layer
- connect components and concepts back to portfolio trackers and maturity models
