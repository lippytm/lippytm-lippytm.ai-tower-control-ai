# Encyclopedia of Everything Applied Model

## Purpose
This document defines a model for turning broad knowledge into applied educational, business, media, robotics, and development systems across the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to make knowledge actionable by connecting references, explanations, lessons, projects, media assets, and operational workflows.

## Core Idea
Move from:
- information
- to structured knowledge
- to learning modules
- to applied projects
- to media and business assets
- to repeatable systems

## Applied Knowledge Layers
### Layer 1 — Reference Layer
Definitions, concepts, examples, and source notes.

### Layer 2 — Explanation Layer
Clear explanations, comparisons, guided walkthroughs, and summaries.

### Layer 3 — Learning Layer
Lessons, pathways, exercises, review prompts, and skill ladders.

### Layer 4 — Project Layer
Code projects, repo tasks, startup systems, robotics workflows, and creative builds.

### Layer 5 — Packaging Layer
Trailers, documentaries, campaigns, ad copy, and business-development assets.

## Core Swarm Roles
### Reference Swarm
Indexes and organizes knowledge.

### Explanation Swarm
Turns references into understandable guidance.

### Learning Swarm
Builds structured lessons and pathways.

### Applied Project Swarm
Connects knowledge to buildable outputs and repo work.

### Packaging Swarm
Transforms knowledge and projects into engaging public-facing and revenue-support content.

## Best Practices
- break large domains into reusable applied modules
- connect every concept to at least one use case or project
- bridge education and business through practical outputs
- reuse one knowledge asset across docs, lessons, repos, and media
- keep the system modular so it can scale over time
