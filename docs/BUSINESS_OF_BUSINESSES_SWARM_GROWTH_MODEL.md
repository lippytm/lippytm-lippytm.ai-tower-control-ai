# Business of Businesses Swarm Growth Model

## Purpose
This document defines a growth model for manufacturing Super Artificial DevOps Synthetic Intelligence Agents and Swarms across the full lippytm Business of Businesses ecosystem.

The goal is to connect repositories, products, services, offers, applications, educational systems, media systems, CRM systems, and reporting systems into one scalable growth architecture.

## Core Vision
Treat the ecosystem as a portfolio of interconnected business surfaces where swarm systems can:
- manufacture new products and services
- improve existing products and services
- expand applications across multiple repos and platforms
- reuse learning, media, CRM, and reporting assets
- create stronger portfolio-wide growth loops

## Growth Layers
### Layer 1 — Foundation Growth
Build architecture, templates, registers, trackers, and profiles.

### Layer 2 — Product and Service Growth
Turn repositories into products, services, educational modules, media assets, and business offers.

### Layer 3 — Cross-System Growth
Reuse components, concepts, campaigns, reporting assets, and story assets across multiple repos and platforms.

### Layer 4 — Portfolio Growth
Coordinate multiple growth surfaces as one portfolio with shared IDs, maturity, scorecards, and backlog systems.

### Layer 5 — Continuous Improvement Growth
Use reasoning, diagnostics, transparency, and documentation systems to improve future waves.

## Swarm Roles
### Product Growth Swarm
Expands existing product and service surfaces.

### Service Packaging Swarm
Turns repo assets into service packages and delivery models.

### Application Growth Swarm
Finds new repo/product/application combinations.

### Portfolio Optimization Swarm
Identifies reuse, overlap reduction, and higher-value rollout opportunities.

### Growth Documentation Swarm
Turns growth opportunities into docs, profiles, scorecards, and tracker updates.

## Suggested Growth Domains
- software and automation products
- educational-business products
- CRM and funnel systems
- media and campaign systems
- reporting and accountability systems
- storytelling and narrative products
- knowledge and encyclopedia products

## Best Practices
- grow by manufacturing repeatable assets, not only one-off outputs
- prioritize surfaces that can support multiple categories at once
- connect every growth idea back to real repo, product, and platform IDs
- use maturity and scorecards to prioritize scaling order
- turn strong documentation and templates into direct growth multipliers
