# R&D Strategy Portfolio Model

## Purpose
This document defines a portfolio-level strategy model for research and development across the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to organize research themes, experimentation priorities, capability growth, prototype promotion, and cross-repo innovation so the ecosystem can grow with more structure and less fragmentation.

## Core Vision
Treat R&D as a strategic portfolio that coordinates:
- research themes
- experiments and prototypes
- capability development
- product/service/application promotion
- cross-repo reuse
- educational, media, CRM, and reporting spillover value

## Strategic Layers
### Layer 1 — Research Theme Layer
Defines major areas of exploration such as:
- DevOps swarm systems
- business-growth applications
- CRM and funnel systems
- learning and robotics systems
- media and narrative systems
- reasoning, diagnostics, transparency, and documentation systems

### Layer 2 — Experiment Layer
Tracks experiment clusters, prototype candidates, and early validation opportunities.

### Layer 3 — Promotion Layer
Identifies what should become a product, service, application, or reusable factory asset.

### Layer 4 — Capability Layer
Maps which capabilities need strengthening to support stronger future waves.

### Layer 5 — Portfolio Optimization Layer
Finds overlaps, reuse opportunities, and multi-area innovations that deserve priority.

## Strategic Questions
For each major R&D theme ask:
- what portfolio value could this create?
- what repo or platform surfaces could benefit?
- what capabilities are required?
- what products or services could emerge?
- what documentation or governance is needed?
- what wave should carry it?

## Best Practices
- prioritize R&D themes that can improve multiple business surfaces
- keep theme definitions broad enough for reuse but clear enough for action
- connect every strategy theme to real inventory and portfolio objects
- use scorecards, maturity, and promotion evidence to avoid premature scaling
- revisit strategy themes after each rollout wave
