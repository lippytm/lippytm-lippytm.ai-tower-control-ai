# Portfolio Monetization Architecture

## Purpose
This document defines a portfolio-level monetization architecture for the lippytm ecosystem.

The goal is to connect repositories, products, services, applications, educational assets, media assets, CRM systems, and AI solutions into clearer business pathways that can support stronger portfolio growth.

## Core Vision
Treat the full portfolio as a connected value system where one repo or asset can contribute to:
- direct products
- service delivery
- applications
- educational-business offers
- media and campaign support
- CRM and growth workflows
- reusable internal assets that strengthen multiple business paths

## Monetization Layers
### Layer 1 — Core Assets
Repositories, components, concepts, story assets, learning modules, prompts, templates, and workflows.

### Layer 2 — Packaged Assets
Products, services, applications, bundles, educational packages, campaign assets, and internal utilities.

### Layer 3 — Delivery Surfaces
GitHub, websites, dashboards, CRM systems, chat systems, media surfaces, and platform adapters.

### Layer 4 — Measurement and Governance
Catalogs, scorecards, trackers, review levels, transparency notes, and maturity models.

### Layer 5 — Expansion and Reuse
Cross-repo reuse, cross-domain growth, portfolio bundling, and future-wave rollout.

## Suggested Monetization Domains
- software and automation
- AI systems and solutions
- educational-business systems
- CRM and workflow systems
- reporting and accountability systems
- media and narrative systems
- portfolio support utilities

## Best Practices
- map revenue or value paths at the same time as packaging paths
- separate reusable core assets from delivery-specific offers
- prioritize assets that can support more than one monetization category
- keep review level, maturity, and platform fit visible
- use portfolio trackers to decide which paths deserve earlier waves
