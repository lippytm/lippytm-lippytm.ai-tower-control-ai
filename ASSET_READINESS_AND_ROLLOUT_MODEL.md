# Asset Readiness and Rollout Model

This document defines how the wider `lippytm` ecosystem can decide when reusable assets are ready for broader rollout and when they should remain local, experimental, or limited in scope.

## Purpose
Help the ecosystem expand in a more durable way by making rollout decisions clearer for lessons, tracks, packs, kits, guides, and product assets.

## Core Principle
Not every asset should be rolled out widely at the same time. Broader rollout should happen when an asset is clear, supportable, reviewable, and mature enough to reduce confusion instead of spreading it.

## Readiness Levels

### 1. Experimental Readiness
Use for:
- early sandbox materials
- early workflow notes
- local lesson drafts
- narrow specialization tests

### 2. Local Reuse Readiness
Use for:
- repeated local use
- small team or repo-specific reuse
- repeated setup success
- clearer review notes

### 3. Cross-Repo Reuse Readiness
Use for:
- patterns that work in more than one repo
- packs that reduce repeated friction
- guides that help multiple tracks
- stronger reusable defaults

### 4. Broad Rollout Readiness
Use for:
- template-ready assets
- pack-ready assets
- member-facing materials
- affiliate-ready materials
- product-ready learning systems

## Rollout Signals
Roll out more broadly when:
- setup notes are stable
- review and security notes are visible
- repeated use shows the asset is durable
- support expectations are clear
- weaker overlapping versions can be simplified or archived

## Best Practices
- keep rollout decisions tied to health checks and review loops
- roll out gradually instead of assuming every reusable asset is immediately broad-ready
- preserve experimental space while clarifying mature reusable paths
- simplify overlapping rollout candidates before multiplying them further
- connect broad rollout back to support and refresh expectations
