# Asset Family and Grouping Model

This document defines how the wider `lippytm` ecosystem can group reusable assets into clearer families as the system expands across learning, practice, workflows, support, packaging, products, memberships, affiliates, and specialization tracks.

## Purpose
Make large numbers of reusable assets easier to navigate by grouping related items into clearer families instead of treating every asset as a completely separate object.

## Core Principle
A growing ecosystem becomes easier to understand when reusable assets are grouped by function, maturity, and reuse role.

## Asset Families

### 1. Learning Family
Use for:
- lessons
- study tracks
- teaching guides
- curriculum maps

### 2. Practice Family
Use for:
- practice loops
- sandbox paths
- repeatable exercises
- builder review routines

### 3. Support Family
Use for:
- setup guides
- toolkit notes
- troubleshooting notes
- environment notes

### 4. Workflow Family
Use for:
- workflow guides
- agent patterns
- secure workflow notes
- runbook-like structures

### 5. Product Family
Use for:
- packs
- kits
- bundles
- member-facing assets
- affiliate-ready assets

## Grouping Rules
Each asset family should make it easier to see:
- what role the asset plays
- what audience it serves
- what maturity level it has
- what reuse path it belongs to
- what support or review expectations apply

## Best Practices
- group assets only when the grouping reduces confusion
- preserve useful specialization inside the family structure
- connect grouping back to catalog, readiness, and promotion guidance
- archive weak or outdated grouped assets clearly
- promote strong repeated grouped assets into clearer reusable packs or standards
